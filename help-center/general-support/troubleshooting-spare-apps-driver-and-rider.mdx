---

Spare Labs consistently enhances and refines its apps to ensure they deliver the intended functionality at the core of our services to customers. This guide outlines the essential troubleshooting steps to follow when the app encounters unusual failures.

<Info>
Try these troubleshooting steps in order, as the most common solutions are listed first for each platform.
</Info>

## iOS Devices (iPhone/iPad)

### Reinstalling the App

<Steps>
  <Step title="Uninstall the App">
    1. Locate the app on your home screen or App Library
    2. Press and hold the Spare app icon until it wiggles or shows options
    3. Tap the **X** on the app icon to delete it
    4. Confirm the deletion if prompted
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Reinstall from App Store">
    1. Open the App Store
    2. Tap on your account icon
    3. Go to **Purchased** or **My Purchases**
    4. Locate the Spare app and tap the download icon (cloud symbol)
    5. Wait for the app to download and install
    6. Once installed, open the app from the home screen
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Connectivity/Network Issues

<Accordion>
  <AccordionItem title="Check Signal Strength">
    Ensure you have a strong cellular signal. If not, move to an open area or try again later.
  </AccordionItem>
  
  <AccordionItem title="Restart Device">
    Restart your iPhone to refresh network settings.
  </AccordionItem>
  
  <AccordionItem title="Verify Carrier Coverage">
    Check your carrier's coverage map to ensure you're in an area with service.
  </AccordionItem>
</Accordion>

### Verify App and Device Versions

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="iOS Version" icon="mobile">
    1. Navigate to **Settings**
    2. Select **General**
    3. Access the **About** section
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="App Version" icon="download">
    1. Locate the app on your device's home screen
    2. Press and hold the app icon until a menu appears
    3. Tap on **App Info** or **Version** (may vary by iOS version)
    4. The app's version information will be displayed
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Android Devices

### Reinstalling the App

<Steps>
  <Step title="Uninstall the App">
    1. Open **Settings**
    2. Go to **Apps** (or **Application Manager**)
       <Note>The location may vary based on your device</Note>
    3. Look for "Apps," "Apps & notifications," or "Application Manager"
    4. Scroll through the list or search for the Spare app
    5. Select the app, then tap the **Uninstall** button
    6. Confirm the action if prompted
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Reinstall from Google Play Store">
    1. Launch the Google Play Store app
    2. Use the search bar to find the Spare app
    3. On the app's page, tap the **Install** button
    4. Review the app's permissions and tap **Accept** or **Install**
    5. The app will be downloaded and installed automatically
    6. Find the app on your home screen or app drawer and open it
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Clear Cache

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access App Settings">
    1. Navigate to the **Settings** app
    2. Go to **Apps** (or **Application Manager**)
    3. Scroll through the list or search for the Spare app
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Clear Cache Data">
    1. Select the Spare app
    2. Tap on the **Storage** option
    3. Tap **Clear Cache** to delete the cached data
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Connectivity/Network Issues

<Accordion>
  <AccordionItem title="Check Signal Strength">
    Ensure you have a strong cellular signal. If not, move to an open area or try again later.
  </AccordionItem>
  
  <AccordionItem title="Restart Your Phone">
    Power off your phone, wait a few seconds, and then restart it.
  </AccordionItem>
  
  <AccordionItem title="Contact Your Carrier">
    Call your mobile carrier's customer support for assistance, especially if the issue persists.
  </AccordionItem>
</Accordion>

### Verify App and Device Versions

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Android Version" icon="android">
    1. Tap the **Settings** app
    2. Swipe down to find and select **About Phone** or **System**
    3. Look for **Android Version** and tap on it
    4. The screen will show your Android version number
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="App Version" icon="google-play">
    1. Launch the **Google Play Store** app
    2. Tap on your profile picture or initials (top right corner)
    3. Select **My apps & games** from the menu
    4. Switch to the **Installed** tab to find the app
    5. The version number will be displayed next to the app name
  </Card>
</CardGroup>
