### üî≥ Printable QR Code Generator for Parts

**What's New**  
You can now generate and print QR code labels for your inventory parts directly from the platform. This helps streamline inventory management and part check-out processes in the workshop.

**Pre-requisites**
- Requires Admin access to Enterprise Asset Management

<Accordion title="See More">

#### Where You'll See It
- *Enterprise Asset Management > Parts*

#### What You Can Do
- From the Parts list, click the new **"Generate Barcodes"** button to open the generator.
- Select a location and, optionally, specific parts for which to create labels.
- Customize the label dimensions (width and height in inches) to fit your label stock.
- Download a printable PDF. Each label includes the part name, its location in the stockroom (bin), a QR code, and a scannable UPC.

#### Why This Matters
Manually creating labels for inventory is time-consuming and error-prone. This feature automates the process, ensuring every part has a scannable, standardized label that links directly back to your EAM system, improving accuracy and efficiency.
</Accordion>

### üñ±Ô∏è Open EAM List Items in a New Tab

**What's New**  
You can now hold `Ctrl` (or `Cmd` on Mac) and click on rows in Enterprise Asset Management (EAM) lists to open the item's detail page in a new browser tab. This brings EAM lists in line with the behavior of other tables across the platform.

**Pre-requisites**
- Requires Admin access
- No feature flags required

<Accordion title="See More">

#### Where You'll See It
- *EAM > Assets* (table rows)
- *EAM > Issues* (table rows)
- *EAM > Parts* (table rows)
- *EAM > Service Reminders* (table rows)

#### What You Can Do
- Perform a normal click on any row to navigate to the detail page in the current tab.
- Hold `Ctrl` or `Cmd` and click a row to quickly open that item's detail page in a new tab for easy comparison or multitasking.

#### Why This Matters
Previously, all clicks on EAM list items would navigate in the same tab, forcing you to lose your place in the list. This update streamlines workflows by allowing you to open multiple items for reference without navigating away from the main list view.
</Accordion>

### ‚õΩÔ∏è View Asset-Specific Energy Entries

**What's New**  
A new "Energy Entries" tab is now available on the asset detail page. This tab appears automatically for any asset belonging to an asset type that has been configured to track energy, providing a centralized place to view its energy history.

**Pre-requisites**
- Requires Admin access
- No feature flags required

<Accordion title="See More">

#### Where You'll See It
- *EAM > Assets > (select an asset)* (new "Energy Entries" tab)
- *EAM > Energy Entries > Add Energy Entry* (form fields)

#### What You Can Do
- View a filtered list of all energy entries for a specific asset directly from its detail page.
- Click the "Add Energy Entry" button from the tab, which pre-fills the form with the current asset.
- Notice that the "Meter Entry" field on the energy entry form now only appears if the selected asset's type is configured for meter readings.

#### Why This Matters
Tracking energy consumption for a specific vehicle or asset was difficult, requiring you to search and filter the main Energy Entries list. This change provides a dedicated, filtered view on the asset's detail page, making it much easier to monitor an individual asset's energy usage.
</Accordion>

### üóìÔ∏è Clearer Wording for Future Out-of-Service Dates

**What's New**  
The information banner on an asset's detail page now uses clearer, context-aware language to indicate if an asset's out-of-service date is in the past or scheduled for the future.

**Pre-requisites**
- Requires Admin access with permissions for Enterprise Asset Management

<Accordion title="See More">

#### Where You'll See It
- *EAM > Assets > (select an asset) > Lifecycle tab*: The information banner at the top of the view.

#### What You Can Do
- When an out-of-service date is set for the future, the banner will now read: "This asset **will be** taken out of service on [Date]".
- For past or current dates, the banner remains: "This asset **has been** taken out of service on [Date]".

#### Why This Matters
Previously, the banner always used past-tense wording, which could be confusing for assets scheduled to be taken out of service in the future. This change improves clarity and reduces misinterpretation of an asset's active status.
</Accordion>

### üîß "General" Tasks to Bypass Part Recommendations

**What's New**  
You can now mark a Service Task as "General." When a technician adds a general task to a Work Order, the system will not show historical part recommendations, streamlining the workflow for catch-all or miscellaneous tasks.

**Pre-requisites**
- Requires Admin access
- No feature flags required

<Accordion title="See More">

#### Where You'll See It
- *EAM > Service Tasks > [Edit a Task]* (a new "General" checkbox is available).
- *EAM > Work Orders > [Create/Edit a Work Order]* (when adding a line item, a hint appears for general tasks).

#### What You Can Do
- Edit a Service Task and check the **General** box to designate it as a catch-all task.
- When this task is added to a work order, technicians will see a hint: "No parts recommendations, this is for catch all tasks."

#### Why This Matters
Not all maintenance tasks have predictable part requirements. This feature allows you to create general service tasks (e.g., "General Inspection," "Miscellaneous Repair") without triggering unnecessary part recommendations, making the work order creation process faster.
</Accordion>
